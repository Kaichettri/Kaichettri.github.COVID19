OTSIMO

Educate the disabled!!

Otsimo Special Education enables the individuals to engage their motor and cognitive skills through assistive matching, drawing, choosing, ordering and sound games.
For individuals or children who struggle with speech and language, Otsimo includes free AAC (Alternative and Augmentative Communication). It is a common practice that is used in speech therapy and special education schools. AAC allows the individual to communicate their thoughts and emotions to their friends and family.
Otsimo games are developed according to the Applied Behavior Analysis - ABA therapy which is the most widely known and trusted early intensive behavioral therapy technique for helping individuals who have learning disorders and attention deficit problems.
Otsimo Special Education gives you plenty of reasons to try the games out:
A learning path feature, that is ideal for children learning new skills. The app provides a path of exercises depending on the level of progress.Featured for more than 250 days! Otsimo is a certified and awarded educational game application developed for individuals diagnosed with learning disorders, attention deficit, autism, down syndrome, aspergers, and other special needs. Created under the guidance of parents, psychologists and special education teachers; assistive games in Otsimo teach fundamental education and concepts about core skills such as:
- Vocabulary and words, - Numbers,
- Emotions,
- Colors,
- Music
- Animals,
- Vehicles and more.
Homeschool category that gives you all the exercises ideal for early education at the comfort of your home.
Game and difficulty settings are completely customizable depending on performance levels.
Otsimo Special Education follows a strict no-ads policy preventing any kind of intrusion and unwanted disturbance.
Parents can have access to detailed report cards that give insight into performance and progress rates.
Can be used by individuals having Autism spectrum disorder, down syndrome, asperger’s, ADHD, cerebral palsy, amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, motor neuron disease (MND), speech impediments, and aphasia.
